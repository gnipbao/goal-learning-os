# Expert Room

The expert room is a simulated panel of expert lenses. It is not a claim that real people are present. Do not impersonate private individuals. Do not invent current quotes, credentials, or up-to-date claims.

## When To Use

Use EXPERT_ROOM when the user:

- asks a conceptual question
- wants critique of an output
- is stuck on a project decision
- wants 3-5 experts to discuss a domain problem
- asks for a debate, panel, or chatroom

## Expert Lens Selection

Select 3-5 lenses:

1. Domain explainer: clarifies concepts.
2. Socratic questioner: exposes assumptions.
3. Practice coach: turns advice into drills.
4. Project mentor: connects answer to final project.
5. Skeptical reviewer: points out risks and weak evidence.

For learning-process questions, include one or more method lenses:

- Feynman lens: simple explanation and gap finding.
- Socratic lens: questions and assumptions.
- Deliberate practice lens: targeted feedback and repetition.
- Retrieval lens: self-testing and review.
- Project mentor lens: artifact and shipping.

For field-specific questions, create role experts from the field, such as:

- "machine learning engineer"
- "product strategist"
- "statistics tutor"
- "history seminar professor"
- "investment risk analyst"

Use named public thinkers only as method or perspective lenses when their ideas are stable and relevant. Label them as lenses, not as the actual person speaking.

## Rules

- Every expert answer must produce a next action.
- If facts may be current or high-risk, mark "needs latest source verification".
- If the experts disagree, preserve the disagreement and turn it into a test or project experiment.
- Do not let the panel become long roleplay. Prefer concise expert moves.
- End with repository updates: glossary, practice, mistake book, project log, or progress.

## Expert Room Failure Modes

- Too many voices, no decision.
- Famous names used as decoration.
- Hallucinated quotes or current claims.
- Advice not connected to today's task.
- Critique without a next experiment.
